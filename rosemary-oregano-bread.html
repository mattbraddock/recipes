Rosemary & Oregano Bread

Ingredients

Roux

  • 50 grams bread flour
  • 75 grams boiling water

Preferment

  • 200 grams bread flour
  • 175 grams cool water
  • 1/8 tsp instant yeast

Dough

  • 150 grams double zero flour
  • 100 grams semolina flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp crushed rosemary
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 150 grams lukewarm water

Wash

  • Extra virgin olive oil

Directions

  • Pour the boiling water over the flour and mix, then refrigerate for 12-16 hours
  • Mix together the flour, water, and yeast for the preferment until the flour is hydrated, then cover and rest for 12-16 hours
  • After the resting period, gently mix together the dry dough ingredients, then add all other ingredients, roux and preferment included, and mix until a shaggy mess
  • Turn the dough out onto a clean surface and knead using the stretch and fold technique until gluten is well developed, up to 15 minutes of constant work
  • Form the dough into a ball, place in a lightly greased bowl, then cover and rest for 45 minutes
  • Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and perform a fold, then cover and rest for 45 minutes
  • Repeat the previous step
  • Shape the dough into a boule and place in a proofing basket, then cover with linen for 40 minutes
    • As the resting period begins, preheat oven to 450F and put a Dutch oven inside
  • Transfer the dough to parchment paper and move it to the preheated Dutch oven; spray the dough with a mist of water and slash the top with a lame, then insert 2-3 ice cubes outside the parchment paper and immediately close the lid
  • Bake for 20 minutes with the lid on, then remove the lid and continue baking for 20 minutes
  • Transfer the dough to a cooling rack and brush with extra virgin olive oil

Notes

  • An additional sheet of parchment paper can be placed at the bottom of the Dutch oven to reduce the odds the bottom burns; alternatively, a baking sheet could be placed on the rack below the Dutch oven
